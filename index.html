About

A brief introduction and focus areas.

I am a junior architect based in Madaba, Jordan, with a Bachelor of Arts in Architecture from Al-Balqa’a Applied University (2019–2024). My work combines academic design studios, competition entries, and real on-site supervision experience. I have trained and worked with several architectural offices, including CUBE Architects, the Office of Rashad Alahmad, and the Office of Alshakatreh & Aljariri under the Jordan Engineers Association training program. I also worked as a Site Supervisor with the United Nations Office for Project Services (UNOPS), supervising maintenance and renovation works within an intensive labour project.

These experiences helped me develop a balanced understanding of both design and execution – from drawings and 3D models to site reports, coordination, and quality control. I enjoy working on projects that are contextual, socially aware, and technically clear.

Selected Projects

A mix of academic, competition, and professional work.

Academic • Healthcare
Healthcare Facility
Design Studio | Lead Designer

A comprehensive healthcare facility designed to provide patient-centered care in a calm, legible environment. The project focuses on clear circulation, natural light, and a healing atmosphere, while integrating sustainable strategies in the building envelope and layout.

Concept • Infrastructure
EV Charging Station
Concept Design | Lead Designer

A modular electric vehicle charging station designed as a safe, accessible, and user-friendly stop for drivers. The project combines clear wayfinding, shaded waiting areas, and integrated fast-charging technologies within a simple, adaptable architectural language.

Academic • Innovation
Technology Innovation Centre
Concept | Lead Designer

A dynamic hub designed to foster creativity, collaboration, and technological advancement. The centre provides flexible workspaces, workshops, and learning areas for students, startups, and young professionals, encouraging knowledge exchange and community engagement.

Graduation • Competition
MAKAN – Graduation Project
Shortlisted | Community-oriented design

A graduation project exploring how architecture can support community, identity, and public life. The design responds to local context and climate while offering shaded, open, and interconnected spaces that invite people to gather, interact, and express themselves.

Professional • Field
UNOPS – Site Supervision
Site Supervisor | Intensive Labour Project

Supervision of maintenance and renovation works under the Intensive Labour Project with UNOPS. Responsibilities included monitoring on-site progress, ensuring safety and quality standards, and preparing daily bilingual reports and documentation while coordinating between contractors, engineers, and UNOPS staff.

Competition • Product
Seat Design – Anamel
Winner | Anamel Jordanian Association

A competition-winning bench design that combines contemporary form with local craftsmanship. The project explores comfort, material honesty, and how a simple seating element can create moments of pause and connection in public space.
